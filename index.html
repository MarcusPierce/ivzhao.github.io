About & Contact

I'm Ivan Zhao, currently in my mid-20s. In my university years I set my mind on pushing the frontiers of cognitive science. However, after a close contact with the academia (and its bureaucratic nature), I have redirected my attention to the web.

I like design, especially design with clarity. It doesn't necessarily imply minimalism (human vision systems have evolved to handle messy environments); instead I think the goal is to build a clear path between the screen and the views' perception - an information highway you may say. In order to do that I draw inspirations from the crafts of typography & cartography and the sciences of visual perception.

I spend most of my days coding, primarily in Python, Cocoa/Objective-C, Javascript/Coffeescript, Google App Engine, HTML/CSS/jQuery, and occasionally in C++ & PostScript. I appreciate programs that are modular, lightweight, and as readable as an essay.

For fun I like to play basketball or listen to the East Village Radio. My imaginary mentors are Wittgenstein, Robert Bringhurst, Jeff Hawkins and Paul Graham.
